I’m a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at UT Arlington, advised by Won Hwa Kim. Before I came to UT Arlington, I graduated from University at Buffalo, SUNY (UB) with a Master's degree majoring in computer science and Beijing University of Posts and Communications (BUPT) with a Bachelor's degree majoring in electronic engineering.

Research Interests:
Deep learning, computer vision and medical imaging. My most recent project is weakly-supervised semantic segmentation which only utilizes class-level labels to implement pixel-level semantic segmentation.

In the past two years, I experienced the classification task in medical imaging (i.e., Alzheimer's disease classification with brain connectivities), deep clustering methods, and graph convolutional networks. Besides those topics, I also have an understanding/experience of the state-of-the-art deep learning models (e.g., FCN, UNet in image segmentation, R-CNN, Fast R-CNN, Faster R-CNN in object detection, backbone networks such as VGG, ResNet used in image classification tasks, VAE and GAN in generative models).

Selected papers [full list]

  1. IPMI
    Enriching Statistical Inferences on Brain Connectivity for Alzheimer’s Disease Analysis via Latent Space Graph Embedding
    Xin Ma, Guorong Wu, Seong Jae Hwang, Won Hwa Kim
    The 27th international conference on Information Processing in Medical Imaging (IPMI), 2021
    Abs

    Tremendous recent literature show that associations between different brain regions, i.e., brain connectivity, provide early symptoms of neurological disorders. Despite signiﬁcant efforts made for graph neural network (GNN) techniques, their focus on graph nodes makes the state-of-the-art GNN methods not suitable for classifying brain connectivity as graphs where the objective is to characterize disease-relevant network dysfunction patterns on graph links. To address this challenge, we propose Multi-resolution Edge Network (MENET) to discover the most disease-speciﬁc connectomic benchmarks with high discrimination power across diagnostic categories. Speciﬁcally, the backbone of MENET is a novel graph edge-wise transform that we propose, which allows us to capture "connectomic" features in a multi-resolution fashion. Using a rich set of the connectomic features, we devise a supervised graph learning framework to jointly select discriminative edges and assign diagnostic labels for graphs. Experiments on two real datasets show that MENET can accurately predict diagnostic labels and identify speciﬁc brain connectivity highly associated with brain disorders such as Alzheimer’s Disease (AD) and Attention-Deﬁcit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD).

  2. ISBI Oral
    Enriching Statistical Inferences on Brain Connectivity for Alzheimer’s Disease Analysis via Latent Space Graph Embedding
    Xin Ma, Guorong Wu, Won Hwa Kim
    IEEE 17th International Symposium on Biomedical Imaging (ISBI), 2020
    Abs PDF Code

    We develop a graph node embedding Deep Neural Network that leverages statistical outcome measure and graph structure given in the data. The objective is to identify regions of interests (ROIs) in the brain that are affected by topological changes of brain connectivity due to specific neurodegenerative diseases by enriching statistical group analysis. We tackle this problem by learning a latent space where statistical inference can be made more effectively. Our experiments on a large-scale Alzheimer's Disease dataset show promising result identifying ROIs that show statistically significant group differences separating even early and late Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) groups whose effect sizes are very subtle.
